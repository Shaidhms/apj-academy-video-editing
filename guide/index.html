Part 1 · VPN Setup

Why a VPN matters for video apps in India

CapCut has been officially banned in India since 2020. The app does not appear on Indian Play Store, App Store, or Microsoft Store searches, and the desktop version refuses to run if it detects an Indian IP. A VPN solves this by routing your traffic through a server in another country, making the app think you are abroad.

Banned in India VPN required Personal use safe

You'll need a VPN for these reasons

  • Initial install — the app stores hide CapCut in India until your store region is changed AND a VPN is active.
  • First launch — CapCut PC throws a Network Error on Indian IPs even after install.
  • Some Pro features and stock content libraries are geo-blocked.
ℹ️
Individual users have not been prosecutedThe ban is on app distribution, not personal use. Still, prefer a reputable VPN over random free APKs to stay safe.

Recommended free / freemium VPNs

🛡️
Proton VPN
Open source, free tier, no logs, no ads. Best choice. Used in this guide.
💨

10 GB free per month, multiple regions, fast.
⚡
Hotspot Shield
Fast, popular, free tier with ads.
🌐
Urban VPN
Completely free, lightweight, browser extension also.

Installing Proton VPN on Android

1

Download Proton VPN from the Play Store

Open Google Play Store → search Proton VPN → tap Install → tap Open.

2

Create a free Proton account

Tap Create account → enter username and a strong password (email optional but recommended). You're auto-signed-in to the Free plan with unlimited data on USA, Netherlands, and Japan servers.

3

Allow VPN connection permission

Tap Quick Connect → Android pops up "Connection request" → tap OK. A small key icon appears in the status bar — that means the VPN is active.

4

Connect to a server in the US, UK or Netherlands

Tap United States (or any free flag) → wait for the green Connected text → open whatismyipaddress.com to verify your country shows abroad.

💡
Tip — launch order mattersAlways launch the VPN BEFORE opening CapCut, Play Store, or any geo-restricted app. Switching the VPN on after often does not help — close and re-open the target app.

2

Block leaks (optional)

Keyframes for animation

A keyframe is a marker that says: at this moment, this property has this value. Two keyframes with different values = animation. CapCut auto-generates the smooth motion in between.

Zoom-in animation

1

Click the clip on the timeline. The right-side properties panel appears.

2

Move the playhead to the start of the clip.

3

In the right panel under Basic, find the diamond icon next to Scale. Click it — it turns orange. First keyframe at 100%.

4

Move the playhead 2 seconds forward.

5

Change Scale to 120%. CapCut auto-adds a second keyframe.

6

Press Space — the clip slowly zooms from 100 to 120.

Fade-in animation

1

Move playhead to start of clip.

2

Set Opacity = 0% → click the diamond.

3

Move playhead 1 sec forward → set Opacity to 100%.

Mixing voice with background music — volume ducking

1

Set base levels

Voice peaks around -6 dB. Music around -18 dB.

2

Manual ducking with keyframes

On the music track, add a Volume keyframe at 0 dB just before the voice starts → another keyframe at -10 dB right after voice begins → reverse on the way out. Music dips while voice is speaking.

3

Or use Auto Volume

Right-click the music track → Auto Volume. CapCut auto-ducks the music whenever it detects speech on another track.

🎙️
Better voice in 30 secondsRecord in a small room with soft surfaces (carpet, curtains, bed) — bathrooms and big rooms echo. Stand 6 inches from the mic. Speak slightly louder than normal conversation. After recording, in the right panel apply Voice enhance for a one-click cleanup.

Beat sync — match images and clips to music

Beat-sync is the single biggest reason short-form videos feel "professional." When your cuts hit on the beat, the brain reads it as polished. CapCut PC has automatic beat detection (yellow markers on the timeline) that does the hard work for you.

Auto-detection works PC + Mobile Needs music track first

Step 1 — Drop your music on the timeline first

Beat detection only works on a track that exists. Bring in your song before adding clips.

1

Audio tab → pick a track, or Ctrl+I to import your own.

2

Drag onto the audio row below your video timeline.

Step 2 — Run automatic beat detection

1

Select the audio track

Click on your music track on the timeline.

2

Click Auto-Beat (or right-click → Beat Detection)

On the toolbar above the timeline you'll see Auto-Beat. Or right-click the audio → Beat Detection.

3

Pick Beat 1 or Beat 2

Beat 1 = main beats only (sparse, dramatic cuts). Beat 2 = every beat including off-beats (faster, busier). Pick based on the energy you want.

4

Yellow dots appear

CapCut scans the audio and drops a yellow marker at every detected beat. Zoom into the timeline to see them clearly.

⌨️
Manual beat marking — press MIf auto-detection misses subtle beats, you can tap them yourself. Play the song and press M on every beat you hear. Each press drops a manual marker. Mix this with auto-detection for hybrid control.

Step 3 — Snap photos / clips to the beats

1

Drop your photos or clips

Drag your images / clips onto the video track above the music. Each one becomes a separate segment.

2

Trim each clip to the beat

Drag the right edge of each photo/clip until its end snaps to the next yellow beat marker. CapCut snaps automatically when you get close.

3

Set default photo duration to match

If you have many photos, click File → Settings → Editing → Default photo duration → set to a value that matches your average beat interval (e.g. 0.5 sec for fast music).

4

Add transitions on the beat

Drop a transition (Whip, Zoom, Glitch) between two clips. Position it directly on a beat marker. The transition itself becomes the beat hit.

Step 4 — Add visual punch on the drops

For the big drops, layer effects keyframed to the beat:

  • Scale punch — keyframe Scale 100% just before the beat, 110% on the beat, back to 100% right after. Adds a heartbeat pulse.
  • Shake — apply the Shake video effect for a few frames around the drop.
  • Flash — drop a white solid for 1-2 frames on the heaviest beat.
  • Speed ramp — slow down before the drop, snap to normal speed on the drop.

Photo slideshow workflow (montage style)

1

Music first → run beat detection.

2

Select all your photos in the Media bin → drag them all to the timeline at once.

3

Trim each to the beat (or set a uniform duration that matches the BPM).

4

Apply Ken Burns — for each photo, add a Scale keyframe (100% start, 110% end). Adds movement to static images.

5

Add transitions on every cut. Same transition (right-click → Apply to all) keeps it consistent.

6

Color-grade so all photos feel like they belong to the same set.

🎯
Pick songs with clear beatsMumbling lo-fi or ambient pads will not auto-detect well. Use songs with prominent kicks and snares — EDM, hip-hop, dance, drum-led pop. The clearer the beat, the better the auto-detection.

Text masking — video inside text

One of the most reused effects on Reels and TikTok. The video plays only inside the letters of your text.

Beginner-friendly Works on PC + Mobile
1

New project, import the video

Drag your background video to the timeline.

2

Add huge bold text

Click Text → Add default text. Type a short word like BOLD or your name. Use a thick font (Impact, Anton, Bebas, Anton). Make font huge — 200 pt+. Stretch the text bar to cover the full clip.

3

Convert to overlay / use Mask

Method 1 (Mask): Right-click the text → Convert to overlay. Apply Mask using the text shape as the mask source.

Method 2 (Chroma key): Make the text a flat green color, place a duplicate of your video above it on the timeline, then apply Chroma Key to the top video so it cuts out the green text shape.

4

Adjust strength and shadow

Inside the Chroma Key panel, adjust Strength and Shadow to at least 50 — until only the video shows inside the letters.

5

Stylize

Add a thick stroke or a colored background underneath. Animate the text in with a slide or scale keyframe.

⚠️
Always go thick and boldThin fonts do not work for text masking. The visible video area becomes too narrow and the effect is lost. Use Impact, Anton, Bebas, or any heavy display font.

Walk-behind text reveal (advanced)

This is the viral effect where text appears as if it is behind a person walking past. CapCut's auto background remover makes this dramatically easier than it used to be.

Advanced Works best on Mobile (auto BG remove)

The full workflow

1

Film the shot

Use a tripod. Film yourself walking past the camera for ~10 seconds. Then film the same scene WITHOUT you in it (a "clean plate") for 5 seconds. The static camera is critical.

2

Build the base layer

Open CapCut → New project → import the walking footage → drop on the timeline.

3

Add the text layer

Add your text in big, bold style. Stretch it to fill the entire walking shot duration.

4

Export and re-import

Export the project as a flat video. Re-import it back into CapCut as a new project. (This bakes the text into the footage so we can mask over it.)

5

Add the walking footage on top

Above the baked video, add the original walking footage as an overlay.

6

Auto remove background

Select the walking overlay → tap Remove BG → Auto removal. CapCut isolates your body from the background.

7

Done

Now your body covers the text as you walk through it. The text appears to be physically behind you.

Manual (no auto-removal) version

If your phone doesn't have Auto Remove BG, do it with masks:

1

Place clip on Track 1, duplicate onto Track 3.

2

Add text on Track 2 between the two video layers.

3

Select Track 3 → Mask → Custom shape.

4

Mask out everything except your silhouette.

5

Use keyframes on the mask Position to follow your body frame by frame as you walk.

📷
Filming tips that matter
• Tripod, no camera shake.
• Plain background — fewer details = cleaner masking.
• Wear something that contrasts with the background.
• Walk slowly and at a steady pace.
• Always shoot a 5-sec clean plate after the take.

Color grading basics

Click Adjust in the right panel. Work the sliders top to bottom.

  • Brightness — pull up if the shot is dark, down if blown out.
  • Contrast — most phone footage is too flat. +10 to +20 is a good starting point.
  • Saturation — too much looks fake. Stay between -10 and +15.
  • Sharpness — only if footage is soft. Max +10.
  • Highlights — pull down to recover sky detail.
  • Shadows — pull up to see dark detail without killing mood.
  • Temperature — warm right for golden hour, cool left for moody.
  • Tint — fix green / magenta color casts.
🖥️
Always check on a bigger screenPhone screens are too saturated. What looks great on your phone often looks flat on a laptop. View the export on the platform you'll publish on (Instagram, YouTube) before sharing.

HSL — the secret to cinematic color

HSL = Hue, Saturation, Lightness. Lets you change one specific color without touching the others. The secret behind the teal-and-orange look.

How to use HSL

1

Select your clip → click Adjust → scroll to HSL.

2

Click on a color circle (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Aqua, Blue, Purple, Pink).

3

Three sliders appear: Hue (shift), Saturation (intensity), Lightness (brightness). Adjust.

4

Repeat for each color you want to control.

Color recipes you can copy

🎬 Teal & Orange — cinematic look
  • Orange: Saturation +15, Lightness +5 (pops skin tones).
  • Yellow: Saturation -20, Hue toward Orange (kills sickly yellows).
  • Aqua: Saturation +10.
  • Blue: Saturation +20, Lightness -10 (deep skies and shadows).
  • Green: Saturation -15, Hue toward Aqua.
🌫️ Moody filmic look
  • Pull Highlights down, Shadows up slightly.
  • Lower overall Saturation by 10-15.
  • HSL — lower Lightness on Reds and Oranges by -10.
  • Push Blues toward Cyan, Lightness -10.
  • Slight Tint to the left for green hint.
🌅 Warm Golden — outdoor / family
  • Temperature +10, Highlights -5, Saturation +10.
  • Use for sunset shots, family content, lifestyle.
⚫ High contrast B&W
  • Saturation -100, Contrast +20, Shadows -10.
  • Use for emotional or moody storytelling.
🌸 Pastel / Soft (beauty / fashion)
  • Saturation -15, Highlights -10, Lightness on skin tones +10.
  • Great for Reels and aesthetic content.
📼 Faded Film (vintage)
  • Use Faded preset, lift Shadows up to 20, lower Contrast by 10.
  • Add film grain at low intensity.
💾
Save as presetIn the Adjust panel, click the Save as preset icon. Apply your recipe to every clip in one click.

The CapCut Pro export trick (free, no watermark)

This is the trick from your Tamil reference video. It uses CapCut's pre-process engine to render Pro effects into a hidden cache folder, which you re-import.

⚠️
Workaround, not a hackPersonal learning use only. Buy CapCut Pro for commercial work — the team behind it deserves the support.

Step 1 — Check version

Open CapCut → click your profile → check the version number. The trick is most reliable on v5.x. Newer versions (7.x+) sometimes encrypt the cache.

Step 2 — Build edit with Pro effects

1

Edit normally — cuts, transitions, text, music.

2

Apply any Pro effects you want (they show a Pro tag — that's fine).

3

Preview to confirm everything looks right.

Step 3 — Compound clip

1

Press Ctrl+A to select all clips, audio, text, and effects.

2

Right-click → Create compound clip.

3

Timeline shows one merged green block.

Step 4 — Apply Motion Blur and pre-process

1

With the compound clip selected, go to the right-side properties panel.

2

Scroll down through the panel until you find Motion Blur.

3

Slide the Motion Blur value all the way to 0.

4

This forces CapCut to pre-process the compound clip with the Pro effects baked in.

5

Wait for the progress bar to reach 100%. Do not move on until it is fully done.

Step 5 — Open the hidden cache folder

1

Press Win+R → type appdata → Enter.

2

In the address bar, replace Roaming with Local → Enter.

3

Navigate to: Local → CapCut → User Data → Projects → com.lveditor.draft → [Project Name] → Resources → Video Alg

4

Inside Video Alg you'll find the .mp4 file with all the Pro features rendered into it (ignore .alpha files).

📁
If AppData is hiddenIn File Explorer: View → Show → Hidden items.

Step 6 — Re-import into CapCut and export

1

Copy the .mp4 from the Video Alg folder to your Desktop.

2

Back in CapCut, press Ctrl+I → import the .mp4.

3

Drag onto the timeline (or onto the compound clip and choose Replace).

4

Click Export → set 1080p or 4K, MP4, 30 or 60 fps.

5

Export. Pro features are baked in, so no watermark, all effects intact.

If it fails

  • Make sure you used the standalone installer, NOT the Microsoft Store version.
  • Try downgrading CapCut to v5.x.
  • Make sure Motion Blur was set to 0 AND pre-processing reached 100% before grabbing the .mp4.
  • Check the Video Alg folder, not Combination — newer versions store the rendered file under Video Alg.
  • If the .mp4 plays without effects, the cache was deleted; redo Step 4 immediately.

Clipchamp vs CapCut — when to use which

Clipchamp is Microsoft's free video editor, pre-installed on Windows 11 and also available as a browser app at clipchamp.com. Microsoft acquired it in 2021.

No India ban No watermark No keyframes No HSL

Key differences from CapCut

🌐
Browser-based
Runs in Chrome/Edge with no install. CapCut PC is a desktop app.
⚠️
Limited keyframes
Animate presets only. Real keyframes need CapCut.
✨
Simpler UI
Easier to learn. Good for quick edits and slideshows.
🔒
No India restrictions
Works without VPN.
🎨
Limited color
Brightness, contrast, saturation, exposure, temperature only. No HSL or masks.
💧
No watermark
Free version exports clean — a real strength.

When to use Clipchamp

  • Quick edits where you do not need keyframes or HSL.
  • Family videos, slideshows, simple corporate clips.
  • If your laptop is low on disk space (browser-based).
  • If you are inside the Microsoft 365 ecosystem already.

When NOT to use it

  • Cinematic color grading — use CapCut.
  • Complex motion graphics with keyframes — use CapCut.
  • Mobile-first editing on phone — use CapCut Mobile.

Keyframes in Clipchamp (and the workaround)

⚠️
Important — Clipchamp does NOT support full keyframe animationYou cannot keyframe Scale, Position, Opacity, Rotation, or Volume manually like in CapCut. This is the single biggest limitation vs CapCut.

Workarounds for animation

  • Use built-in Animate options on text and images (Fade in, Slide in, Zoom in, Bounce). Click your asset → Animate tab → choose preset.
  • For video zooms: split the clip into 2 parts, apply different Crop/Zoom levels via Filters, and put a transition between them.
  • Use Speed ramping (right panel → Speed) to fake dynamic moves.
  • If you really need keyframes, edit in CapCut and re-import the result into Clipchamp.

Common mistakes to avoid

🚫
Skip-the-VPN trap
CapCut PC throws Network Error in India without a VPN active before launch.
🏪
Microsoft Store version
The Pro export trick only works on the standalone .exe from capcut.com.
💫
Too many transitions
One or two styles per video — max.
🌈
Over-saturation
Pushing Saturation and Sharpness too far. Subtle wins.
🎚️
Bad audio levels
Voice peaks ~-6 dB, music ~-18 dB. Always check before exporting.
🅰️
Thin fonts for masks
Use Impact, Anton, Bebas. Thin fonts kill the masking effect.
📌
Accidental keyframes
Deselect the keyframe before changing a property globally.
📱
Phone-screen only
Always check on a laptop. Colors look very different on bigger screens.
